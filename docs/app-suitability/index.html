App Suitability

Life Game is a calm, educational simulator inspired by John Conway’s research into cellular automata.

Purpose & Audience

The experience showcases Conway’s Game of Life: players experiment with grid-based rules to see how simple patterns grow and fade. The app is designed for curious learners, educators, and fans of computational art who enjoy lighthearted discovery at their own pace.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand how Conway’s four rules create emergent patterns such as gliders, pulsars, and Gosper glider guns.
  • Explore mathematical concepts including cellular automata, state transitions, and self-organization.
  • Encourage creativity by stamping well-known patterns or designing new Life structures.

Safety & Privacy

  • No third-party content, advertisements, or external links appear inside the app.
  • Simulations run locally on the device; the app does not require network access to function.
  • Optional diagnostics reporting is controlled by the user and leverages Apple’s privacy protections.

Online Interactions

Life Game is a single-player experience without real-time or asynchronous communication. There is no matchmaking, user-generated content sharing, or multiplayer interaction. All creations remain private on the device unless the player shares screenshots manually.

Tone & Suitability

The app presents a minimalist grid with soft visuals and relaxing pacing, making it appropriate for general audiences. There is no violence, adult content, or rapid-action gameplay—just an inviting sandbox for experimenting with the patterns that made Conway’s work famous.
